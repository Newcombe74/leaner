About LeanER

Canadian hospitals have a history of long wait times for emergency room admission. In recent years this issue has been intensified by the COVID-19 pandemic increasing hospital usage There are many reasons someone may require emergency room services, and not all are immediate, life-threatening emergencies. LeanER was desirned for individuals who need care but face long waits because of comparably less severe ailments. LeanER allows you to spend your wait time outside of the hospital, such as in the comfort of your own home.

How to use LeanER:

Your Account:

The first step is to register or login to your LeanER account. This is where your important health information is stored and used for each emergency room admission you create using LeanER. You can update this information any time using the My Account page.

Select a hospital:

Based on your location you will be provided with a list of nearby hospitals with emergency rooms. You may select one hospital to continue the admission process with. You can decide which hospital you would like to check into based on location or the current average wait time.

Input information on your current issue:

Include important information about your current ailment that will help determine your expected wait time. If you have severe or high-risk symptoms LeanER will alert you to call an ambulance or make your way to the emergency room right away. Otherwise, you will be entered into the queue.

Wait comfortably:

Wait in your desired location and monitor the website for updates on your wait time. It is recommended that you arrive at the hospital 1 hour prior to when you are expected to reach the front of the line. This ensures that you will be ready to receive care as soon as possible. At any time you may exit the queue or use the Emergency 911 button (located at the top right corner of the webpage) to dispatch an ambulance in the case of an emergency.

When to not use LeanER:

Life-Threatening Emergencies:

In cases of severe or potentially life-threatening emergencies it is recommended that you call 911 immediately. This can include suspected heart attacks, strokes, or dangerous, previously-doagnosed conditions. Symptoms that are considered severe include chest pains, difficulty breathing, confusion, heavy blood loss, loss of consciousness, and more.
